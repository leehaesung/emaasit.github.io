Who is Daniel Emaasit?
A Data Geek in Transportation Engineering

Daniel Emaasit is currently a Ph.D student of Civil & Transportation Engineering at University of Nevada Las Vegas (UNLV). He is a graduate research assistant in the Transportation Research Center under the surpervision of Dr. Alexander Paz. His research interests include database design, data mining, advanced analytics, predicitive analytics, machine learning, statistical modeling, cluster analysis, time series, GIS spatial analysis, and business intelligence.

Current Projects:
Database Design and Business Intelligence for Nevada Department of Transportation

My project is a four-year engineering doctorate (EngD) research funded by EPSRC and XP Solutions. The aim is to develop a new decision support framework for drainage design evaluation and optimisation. The project is co-supervised by University of Exeter and XP Solutions under the STREAM research initiative. For more information, see this Prezi.

Software:
Open-Source Projects

I enjoy contributing to the open-source community in my spare time. I am particularly interested in data mining and visualisation projects. My favourite programming languages are R, Matlab and Python. I am also learning how to leverage the power of JavaScript, CSS and HTML5 for web development. My recent projects include:

  • CrimeMap - crime data visualisation.
  • rCrimemap - interactive crime map.
  • heatmapStock - stock market data visualisation.
  • rPlotter - making R even more colourful.
  • blocks - data-driven documents (D3) and code gallery.
  • rugsmaps - Maps of R user groups around the world.

See all my projects and source code on GitHub.
