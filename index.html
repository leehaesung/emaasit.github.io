Who is Daniel Emaasit?
A Data Geek in Transportation Engineering

I am currently a Ph.D student of Civil & Transportation Engineering at University of Nevada Las Vegas (UNLV). I am also a graduate research assistant in the Transportation Research Center under the surpervision of Dr. Alexander Paz. My research interests include database design, data mining, advanced analytics, predicitive analytics, machine learning, statistical modeling, cluster analysis, time series, GIS spatial analysis, and business intelligence.

Current Projects:
Database Design and Business Intelligence for Nevada Department of Transportation

I am currently working on two research projects funded by Nevada Department of Transportation (NDOT). They include;

  • Development of an Enterprise GIS Database for Utilities within the Right-of-Way of Roadways Maintained by the Nevada Department of Transportation.
  • Business Intelligence for Nevada Department of Transportation.


Software:
Open-Source Projects

I am leveraging the currently favored open-source programs to conduct data science. My favorite open-source language is R. I am also learning Python, Java, SQL, MongoDB and Hadoop. I am particularly interested in applying these tools for advanced data analytics and visualisation to extract hidden insights. My recent open-source projects include:

  • Weather - Effects of Severe Weather in US.
  • Health - Activity Monitoring Devices.
  • TidyData - Getting & Cleaning Data.
  • rConcepts - Data Science Concepts.
  • Power - Individual Household Electric Power Consumption.

Review all my projects and source code on GitHub.


Publications and Presentations

I love to conduct research and contribute to the body of knowledge. Some of my research has been published in revered journals and presented at top-tier conferences in the field of Transportation Engineering like Transportation Research Board (TRB). I am also a reviewer in some of these journals. Check out this brief shortlist of my research:

  • Stochastic User Equilibrium - JTT, 2012.
  • Bicycle Crash Injury Severities - JTT, 2012.
  • Median Cable Barrier Crash Frequency - JTSS, 2014.
  • Spatial Autocorrelation - TRB, Washington, D.C., 2013.
  • Pedestrian & Bicycle Safety - TSU, Nashville, TN, 2013.
